# Q13. Arduino Pin Types
## List and explain with examples: Digital Input, Digital Output, Analog Input, PWM Output, and I2C/SPI pins available on Arduino UNO. For each, give one real IoT use case.

## Digital Input (Listening to Yes/No)
### *How it works:* Reads exactly two states: On (5V) or Off (0V).
### *Human view:* Like checking if a door is locked or unlocked.
### *Pins:* 0 to 13.
### *IoT use:* Security window sensor detecting a break-in.

## Digital Output (Telling Yes/No)
### *How it works:* Sends a steady On (5V) or Off (0V) voltage.
### *Human view:* Like flipping a light switch on the wall.
### *Pins:* 0 to 13.
### *IoT use:* Turning on a smart water pump via a relay.

## Analog Input (Reading a Scale)
### *How it works:* Reads varying voltages. Converts them to numbers from 0 to 1023.
### *Human view:* Like reading the exact temperature on a dial thermometer.
### *Pins:* A0 to A5.
### *IoT use:* Checking a farm probe to measure exact soil moisture.

## PWM Output (Faking a Middle Ground)
### *How it works:* Blinks a digital pin hyper-fast to mimic lower voltages.
### *Human view:* Rapidly clicking a flashlight to make it look dim.
### *Pins:* Marked with ~ (3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 11).
### *IoT use:* Controlling a smart ventilation fan's speed.

##  I2C & SPI Pins (Sharing Complex Data)
### *How it works:* Specialized communication lines that send long strings of digital text.
### *Human view:* Passing a detailed written letter instead of just shouting.
### *Pins:* I2C (A4, A5) and SPI (10, 11, 12, 13).
### *IoT use:* Sending live weather text data to a mini OLED display.
