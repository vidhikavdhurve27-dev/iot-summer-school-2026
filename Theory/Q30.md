## How It Works: 
### SDA and SCL LinesI2C (Inter-Integrated Circuit) is a synchronous, multi-device, bidirectional serial communication bus protocol used to link microcontrollers to advanced peripheral modules over short physical distances. It requires only two active wire lines:
### SDA (Serial Data Line): The bidirectional track where the master controller and slave sensors transmit and receive the actual data bits.
### SCL (Serial Clock Line): The dedicated clock line driven entirely by the master unit. It generates regular square pulses that synchronize the timing of data sampling along the SDA rail.

## How I2C Addressing Works
### Every peripheral device connected to the I2C bus has a hardwired or user-configurable unique 7-bit binary address assigned by the manufacturer (e.g., 0x27 for an LCD screen). Because devices share the exact same wire tracks, the master always begins a transaction by broadcasting a frame containing the target device's address. All connected chips listen to the transmission, but only the peripheral that possesses the matching address wakes up to respond, ignoring all subsequent data traffic meant for other nodes.
### 3 Common IoT Sensors that use 
### I2CBMP280 / BME280: Digital barometric pressure and ambient temperature sensor.
### MPU6050: 6-axis Accelerometer and Gyroscope motion tracking chip.
### BH1750: Digital Ambient Light Intensity (Lux) sensor.
